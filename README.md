Qualitative Analysis of Differential Dimensions in CDSS Redeployment
Overview

This repository contains the qualitative study materials and supporting artifacts for research on the redeployment of Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS) across heterogeneous healthcare ecosystems.

The focus of this work is to systematically identify, categorize, and structurally represent differences between healthcare data environments that affect CDSS portability and adaptation.

This study contributes to the development of a Structured Multi-Dimensional (SMD) framework for analyzing interoperability challenges and guiding adaptation strategies.

Research Context

Clinical Decision Support Systems are typically designed for a specific healthcare provider and are tightly coupled with:

Local data schemas

Terminology systems

Measurement conventions

Clinical workflows

Documentation standards

When attempting to redeploy a CDSS to a new healthcare ecosystem, multiple layers of differences emerge. These differences are often overlooked, leading to redevelopment from scratch, increased costs, and reduced scalability.

This repository documents the qualitative investigation conducted to better understand these differential dimensions and their implications for CDSS adaptation.

Study Design

This qualitative study was conducted using:

Structured interview flows

Survey instruments

Participant response analysis

The objective was to capture expert insights regarding:

Types of ecosystem differences

Variable-level mismatches

Representation and metadata inconsistencies

Data distribution shifts

Practical adaptation challenges

The collected insights support the formalization of differential dimensions and adaptation scenarios within the broader CDSS redeployment framework.

Structured Multi-Dimensional Perspective

The qualitative findings support the identification of differential analysis across multiple dimensions, including:

Structural or Shallow differences (existence of variables)

Semantic or Deep differences (conceptual meaning alignment)

Representation differences (units, encoding, value formats)

Metadata differences (measurement context and documentation standards)

Data-level differences (distribution shifts and missingness)

These dimensions collectively influence the feasibility of:

Direct reuse

Transformative adaptation

Reconstruction

Incompatibility determination

Surrogate-based substitution

The qualitative analysis serves as foundational evidence for the structured modeling of these differential outcomes.
